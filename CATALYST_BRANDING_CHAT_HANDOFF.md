## DESIGN EXPLORATION AREAS

### 1. Base Proposal Template
- Header/footer design (logo placement, page numbers, confidentiality notice)
- Section header styling (how do "Learning Objectives," "Needs Assessment," etc. look?)
- Typography system (heading fonts vs body fonts, sizes, weights)
- Color palette application (where do brand colors show up — headers? accents? tables?)
- Table styling (borders, shading, header rows)
- Margins, spacing, overall density

### 2. Format-Specific Variations
Different proposal formats may warrant different layouts:
- **Congress coverage** — may emphasize schedule/agenda formatting
- **Webcast/virtual** — may emphasize technology/platform details
- **Live course** — may emphasize venue/logistics sections
- **Enduring/on-demand** — may emphasize content architecture
- Question: Should these be visually distinct or just structurally different?

### 3. TA-Specific Design Touches
Subtle ways to differentiate therapeutic areas without breaking brand consistency:
- **Color accents** — each TA gets a secondary accent color? (e.g., cardiology = red accent, oncology = purple accent, neurology = blue accent)
- **Section icons** — small TA-relevant icons in headers?
- **Cover page variants** — TA-specific imagery or color blocks on page 1?
- **Or: keep it uniform** — same look regardless of TA, differentiated only by content

### 4. Elevated Options (Beyond Current State)
Ideas that go beyond replicating current proposals:
- Modern, clean design vs traditional medical education look
- Data visualization integration (score summaries, evidence maps)
- Executive summary page with visual KPIs
- QR codes linking to supporting evidence
- Professional cover page with TA-specific imagery

### 5. Master Book Application
If we land on a strong design direction:
- Could the same visual language apply to the Master Book v2.1?
- Cover page, section headers, typography — consistent Catalyst/CAMEOS brand
- This is optional/bonus — don't let it slow down the proposal template decision
